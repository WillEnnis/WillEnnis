## <samp>`> featured_work`</samp>

<details open>
<summary><b>⚡ Electrically Conductive 3D Printing — Undergraduate Research</b></summary>
<br/>

Baylor additive-manufacturing research into how you print electricity, not just plastic. I study how **FFF/FDM layer architecture** — raster angle, print orientation, and carbon-black filler dispersion — governs both **mechanical strength/fracture** and **electrical conductivity** in printed parts.

- **The question:** how do conductive pathways actually form inside a printed part, and how do layer choices help or break them?
- **The work:** design-of-experiments across print parameters, mechanical + electrical characterization, literature synthesis into a sharper research question.
- **Why it matters:** printed sensors, embedded circuits, and multifunctional structures — parts that *do* something, not just hold shape.

`Materials` · `DfAM` · `Design of Experiments` · `Characterization`
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>🤖 AIOS — a personal AI operating system</b></summary>
<br/>

A self-documenting command center for my life and work: a learning assistant that wires into calendar, email, and task systems, plus a structured filesystem I can run projects and future companies on.

- Custom AI **skills/agents** for daily briefs, research, drafting, and weekly reviews.
- A growth loop that logs every lesson and turns repeated manual work into automation.
- Built to grow *with* me — the system edits and improves itself.

`Claude Code` · `Agents` · `Automation` · `Systems design`
</details>

- **Stack:** React Three Fiber / Three.js, GSAP / Framer Motion, modern React UI.
- **Goal:** turn a strong story into a strong *first impression* — substance and presentation, not one without the other.

`Three.js` · `R3F` · `Motion` · `Front-end`
